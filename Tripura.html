Tripura

Tripura


Chief Minister-Manik Saha

Manik Saha is an Indian politician serving as the 11th and current Chief Minister of Tripura since 2022. He represents the Town Bordowali Assembly constituency in the Tripura Legislative Assembly since 2022.

Governor-N. Indrasena Reddy

Nallu Indrasena Reddy is an Indian politician from Telangana. He is the current Governor of Tripura. He is an ex national secretary of the Bharatiya Janata Party. He was earlier president of the United state unit of the party.

Culture and Heritage


Food
Mui Borok

Mui Borok is the traditional food of Tripura. Tripuri cuisines are prepared using Berma (dried and fermented fish) a key ingredient. Bangui rice and fish stews, meat roasts, local herbs along with bamboo shoots and fermented fish are very popular.

Agriculture
Rice

Tripura is the second largest producer of rice in the North-Eastern region after Assam. During 2011-12 the area covered under paddy cultivation in Tripura was 266 thousand ha with production of 718 thousand ton. In Tripura Rice is the principle crop of the state covering 78% of cultivable area.

Festival
Garia Puja

Garia Puja is a week-long festival in Tripura, India that celebrates the deity of livestock and wealth, Lord Garia. It is celebrated in the month of Baisakh (April) and is the biggest festival in the state.

Dress
Rignai Rikutu

The most famous traditional dress of Tripura is called "Rignai" for women and "Rikutu" for men. The Rignai is a traditional attire worn by Tripuri women. It consists of a woven cloth wrapped around the lower body, resembling a sarong or a skirt.

Music
Jaduni

Jaduni, also known as Sarbang, is a traditional folk song from Tripura that is based on love and pleasure. It's an Uttar ragini tune.

Dance
Hojagiri

A dance performed by the Reang community, where four to eight women dance acrobatically, while men provide most of the music. The women sing in chorus and balance objects on their heads and hands.

Architecture
Unakoti

A hill in Unakoti District that features a large collection of stone sculptures and carvings. The name Unakoti means "one less than a crore" and is believed to refer to the number of sculptures on the hill.
