Culture and Heritage


Food
Bamboo steamed fish

A simple yet famous dish that's a staple in Naga homes. The fish is minimally spiced and the bamboo's subtle flavor kicks in for a flavorful explosion in your mouth.

Agriculture
Rice

Rice is staple food of Nagaland with about 86 per cent of the cultivable area in the state under jhum and terrace rice cultivation systems. Traditional rice varieties are grown in altitude ranging from 300 to 2500 metres.

Festival
Hornbill Festival

This annual festival is held from December 1–10 in Kisama Heritage Village near Kohima. It's known as the "Festival of Festivals" because it celebrates the rich cultural heritage of Nagaland's diverse tribes. The festival was started in 2000 by the Government of Nagaland to promote inter-group interaction and protect the state's tribal cultures.

Dress
Shongphong shawls and skirts

To immerse yourself in Nagaland's culture and show respect for its people, consider wearing traditional Naga attire. For men, this typically includes a shawl-like garment called a “shongphong” and a loincloth known as a “joho.” Women often wear vibrant woven shawls and skirts.

Music
Hereileu

The Hereileu song is a war song of Nagaland, a state in the northeastern part of India. It is often sung by the older people of Nagaland to share their stories of past battles.

Dance
Aaluyattu

Aaluyattu is a folk dance form from the state of Nagaland. The dance is performed by the Konyak tribe. Other folk dances of Nagaland are Modse, Agurshikukula, Sadal Kekai, Changai Dance, Kuki Dance, Leshalaptu, Khamba Lim, Mayur Dance, Rengma, Monyoasho, Seecha and Kukui Kucho.

Architecture
Naga Hut

The primary form of traditional architecture in Nagaland is the Naga hut or house, usually constructed using locally available materials like bamboo, wood, thatch, and sometimes stone.
