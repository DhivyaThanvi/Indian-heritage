Culture and Heritage


Food
Pika pila

Pika pila is a traditional pickle from the Apatani tribe of Arunachal Pradesh, India, made from bamboo shoots, pork fat, and king chili. It's a popular accompaniment to many dishes, including rice, dry fish, and curry.

Agriculture
Pulses

Pulses are one of the important food crops globally due to their higher protein content. Pulses are an important group of crops in India, which is also responsible for yielding large financial gains by amounting to a large part of the exports..

Festival
Losar

Losar is a festival celebrated in Arunachal Pradesh that marks the Tibetan New Year. It is celebrated by the Monpa tribe, who are mainly found in the Tawang and West Kameng districts. The festival is observed in February or early March and lasts for two weeks.

Dress
skirt and blouse-Gale and Bakh.

Arunachal Pradesh is known for its distinctive tribal costumes and dresses. The traditional dress for women is the wrap-around skirt and blouse. Men wear a shirt and a trouser called the 'Gale' and 'Bakhi.'

Music
Ja-Jin-Ja

Ja-Jin-Ja: A song that is sung at feasts, weddings, and other social gatherings. Both men and women sing it in chorus or individually

Dance
Buiya

A popular dance performed by the Digaru Mishmis tribe at tribal festivals. People of all ages perform this joyful dance to the beat of drums and gongs, wearing tribal clothing and jewelry. The dance features unique formations and is considered a way to bond people

Architecture
traditional houses

These vary by region, but often include wooden or bamboo pillars, bamboo walls, and roofs made from dry leaves. Stilt kutccha houses, or "chang", are built with bamboo and wood, and thatched roofs. Galo houses are large, square houses built on a platform with a ridged or hipped roof.
