Goa


Chief Minister-Pramod Sawant

Pramod Pandurang Sawant (born Pramod Panduronga Sawant; 24 April 1973) is an Indian politician and ayurveda medical practitioner who has served as the 11th Chief Minister of Goa since March 2019.

Governor-P. S. Sreedharan Pillai

Shri Sreedharan Pillai is a successful lawyer, a former politician, an organizer par excellence, an orator, a prolific writer, a philanthropist and a thinker. Shri P.S. Sreedharan Pillai has held the following positions: State Convenor, Lok Tantric Yuva Morcha.

Culture and Heritage


Food
Bebinca

Created by a resourceful nun in 17th-Century Goa, this sticky, seven-layer cake is made primarily from eggs, coconut, sugar, ghee and flour.

Agriculture
Rice

Rice is the staple food crop of Goa. The crop is cultivated over an area of 42,973 ha with a production of 1,55,818 mt annually. The average productivity of the crop is about 3,399 kg/ha.

Festival
Shigmo festival

Held in March each year, the streets of Goa hum with people and noise when the Shigmo festival begins. This spring festival is all about music, song and dance.

Dress
Pano Bhaju Pudvem

The traditional dress of Goa varies between genders. For women, it's typically the 'Pano Bhaju' paired with a Kunbi Saree, and for men, it's a simple white shirt with half pants known as 'Pudvem' or 'Kashti'.

Music
Mando Suvari

The Mando is a traditional folk music and dance form from Goa that combines Indian and Western traditions. It originated in the 19th and 20th centuries among Goan Catholics, especially in the villages of Curtorim, Margão, Loutolim, and Raia

Dance
Fugdi

A folk dance from the Konkan region, performed by women during Ganesh Chaturthi and Vrata. The dance is rooted in the cultural traditions of Maharashtra and Goa.

Architecture
Bom Jesus cathedral

The three-storied facade of the Church shows Ionic, Doric and Corinthian Orders, and has a main entrance flanked by two smaller ones, each having Corinthian columns supporting a pediment.
