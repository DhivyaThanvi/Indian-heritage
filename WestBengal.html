WestBengal


Chief Minister-Mamata Banerjee

Mamata Banerjee is an Indian politician who is serving as the eighth and current chief minister of the Indian state of West Bengal since 20 May 2011, the first woman to hold the office.

Governor-C. V. Ananda Bose

Dr. C. V. Ananda Bose is an Indian retired 1977-batch IAS officer and politician, who has been serving as the Governor of West Bengal since 23 November 2022.

Culture and Heritage


Food
Macher jhol

Macher jhol is a traditional spicy fish curry from the Bengali and Odia cuisines of eastern India. It's made with a variety of fish, including hilsa, rohu, katla, and pabda, and seasoned with turmeric, garlic, onions, ginger, and other spices.

Agriculture
Rice

An analysis of area, production and yield data show that the productivity of rice in West Bengal has been declining. West Bengal was the top producer at 15.88 million tonnes (mt) with a share of 13.36 per cent in the national output in 2019-20.

Festival
Durga Puja

Durga Puja is a major festival in West Bengal, India that celebrates the Hindu goddess Durga, her victory over the demon Mahishasura, and the triumph of good over evil.

Dress
Dhoti and Kurta,Saree

West Bengal's traditional clothing blends simplicity with sophistication. The Tant Saree is known for its lightness and comfort, while the Baluchari Saree, with its intricate weaving and storytelling motifs, is a work of art. Men typically wear the Dhoti and Kurta, reflecting Bengal's literary and cultural heritage.

Music
Baul

The music of the Bauls, Baul Sangeet, is a particular type of folk song. Their music represents a long heritage of preaching mysticism through songs in Bengal. Bauls pour out their feelings in their songs but never bother to write them down.

Dance
Gambhira

The Gambhira is a traditional folk dance and musical theater form from West Bengal, India that is known for its satirical commentary on social and religious issues.

Architecture
Chala

The Chala style is a style of temple architecture in Bengal that originated in the 15th century. The main features of this style are curved cornices and tops.
