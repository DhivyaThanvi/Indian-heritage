Jharhand


Chief Minister-Hemant Soren

Hemant Soren is an Indian politician from Jharkhand, who is currently serving as Chief Minister of Jharkhand since 4 July 2024. He also served as the 5th Chief Minister of Jharkhand from 2019 to 2024 and from 2013 to 2014.

Governor-Santosh Gangwar

Santosh Kumar Gangwar is an Indian politician currently serving as the Governor of Jharkhand. He is a former Minister of State with independent charge in the Ministry of Labour and Employment in the Government of India.

Culture and Heritage


Food
Rice

The staple foods in Jharkhand are rice, dal, vegetables, and tubers. Some dishes include Chilka Roti, Malpua, Dhooska, Arsa roti, and Pitha. Rugra (a type of mushroom) and bamboo shoots are also used as vegetables.

Agriculture
Traditional seeds

Some traditional seeds used in Jharkhand include arhar (pigeon pea), bajra (pearl millet), gundali (little millet), kodo millet, maize, rice bean, sorghum, and urad (black gram).

Festival
Bhagta Parab

This festival comes between the period of spring and summer. Among the tribal people of Jharkhand, Bhagta Parab is best known as the worship of Budha Baba. People fast during the day and carry the bathing Pahan the priest, to the tribal mandir called Sarana Mandir.

Dress
Bhagwan sarees and cholis

The traditional dress for men in Jharkhand is the bhagwan, a single piece of cloth. Men also wear lungis, sherwanis, and kurtas for festivals. Women wear sarees and cholis, and older women wear a parthan and panchi.

Music
Santhal Music

The music is characterized by its rhythmic complexity and the use of traditional instruments like the 'dhamsa' and 'mandar'. Santhal songs, often accompanied by dance, are performed during various social and religious occasions.

Dance
Jhumair

A popular folk dance performed during harvest festivals. It's known for its graceful movements, intricate footwork, and expressive gestures. The music is created by traditional instruments like the nagada, harmonium, and violin.

Architecture
vernacular houses

Jharkhand predominantly has two different styles of vernacular houses: huts and havelis. These houses were constructed, without any mechanical means, in such a manner as to create micro-climates inside them to provide high thermal comfort levels.
