Haryana

Haryana


Chief Minister-Nayab Singh Saini

Nayab Singh Saini is an Indian politician serving as the 11th and Current Chief Minister of Haryana since 12th March 2024 and the President of the Bharatiya Janata Party, Haryana since 2023.

Governor-Bandaru Dattatreya

Bandaru Dattatreya is an Indian politician serving as the current governor of the State of Haryana since 2021. He was also the 20th governor of Himachal Pradesh and was the member of Lok Sabha for Secunderabad from 2014 to 2019.

Culture and Heritage


Food
kachri ki sabzi

This chutney is made from a vegetable called Kachri that is locally grown in the state. Kachri is a wild variety of cucumber and looks like minute watermelons from outside. Kachri ki chutney is made from ground kachri, garlic, onion, other spices, and yogurt.

Agriculture
wheat

In Haryana wheat is cultivated on 2533 thousand hectare with 11876 thousand ton production contributing to 4678 kg/ha yield. Haryana contributes around 11 percent in the total production of wheat in the country.

Festival
Pinjore Heritage Festival

This annual festival in December celebrates the rich culture, history, and heritage of Pinjore town and its gardens. It features performances by poets, singers, and dancers.

Dress
Daaman, Kurti & Chunder.

Their basic trousseau includes Daaman, Kurti & Chunder. 'Chunder' is the long, coloured piece of cloth, decorated with shiny laces and motifs, and is meant to cover the head. 'Kurti' is a shirt like a blouse. The 'Daaman' is the flairy ankle-long skirt, in striking vibrant colours.

Music
Dholak

A small percussion instrument with a wooden shell and skin-covered heads. The pitch is adjusted by a rope or thread that goes around the shell and over braces.

Dance
Phag

A seasonal dance performed by both men and women, usually in the month of Phalguna. Women wear colorful traditional clothes and men wear colorful turbans.

Architecture
Mughal building

Haryana's Mughal building is known for being grand, symmetrical, and decorated with a lot of delicate details. Most of the buildings were made of red sandstone and white marble, and they had intricate carvings and inlays.
