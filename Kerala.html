Culture and Heritage


Food
Puttu and Kadala Curry

Puttu is extremely popular as a breakfast dish throughout Kerala. It is served with kadala curry, which is made of black chickpeas, garlic, mustard seeds, red chilli, onions, whole coriander, and turmeric, curries leaves, garam masala, salt, and of course, grated coconut.

Agriculture
Coconut

In Kerala, coconut trees are commonly grown in homesteads and on small farms. The state has over 800,000 hectares of coconut plantations, which account for about 41% of India's total coconut production.

Festival
Onam

The official festival of Kerala, Onam is a 10-day harvest festival that celebrates the Hindu deity Vishnu's fifth avatar, Vamana. The festival is marked by floral carpets, rituals, and a large feast called Onasadhya. Onam is celebrated by Malayalis around the world, regardless of caste or religion.

Dress
Mundu Kerala Saree

In Kerala, the traditional dress for engagements is elegant and simple. Women usually wear the 'Kerala Saree' or 'Set Mundu', which is white or cream with a golden border. Men often wear a 'Mundu', a long white or cream cloth worn around the waist, paired with a shirt.

Music
Sopanam Sangeetham

Sopanam Sangeetham is a traditional style of devotional singing and music from Kerala.

Dance
Kathakali

Kathakali is a blend of dance, music and acting and dramatizes stories, which are mostly adapted from the Indian epics. It is a stylised art form, the four aspects of abhinaya angika, aharya,vachika, satvika and the nritta, nritya and natya aspects are combined perfectly.

Architecture
Nalukettu

The traditional style of courtyard dwelling in Kerala, India is known as Nalukettu. The word "nalukettu" comes from the words "nalu" which means four and "kettu" which means halls.
