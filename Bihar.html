Bihar

Bihar


Chief Minister-Nitish Kumar

Nitish Kumar (born 1 March 1951) is an Indian politician who has been serving as the 22nd chief minister of Bihar since 22 February 2015, having previously held the office from 2005 to 2014 and for a short period in 2000. He is Bihar's longest serving chief minister whilst also holding the post for his 9th term.

Governor-Rajendra Vishwanath Arlekar

Rajendra Vishwanath Arlekar is the current governor of Bihar. Former President Zakir Husain and Ram Nath Kovind were two such governors of Bihar who succeeded on to become the president of India. Location of Bihar in India.

Culture and Heritage


Food
Litti chokha

A staple dish of Bihar that's thought to have originated as survival food during the Sepoy Mutiny in 1857. It's made of wheat and sattu, a pulverized version of fried chana dal that's similar to gram flour

Agriculture
Cereals

Rice, wheat, and maize are the main cereal crops grown in Bihar. Bihar is among the top five rice and wheat producers in India and is the leader in rice yield.

Festival
Chhath Puja

An ancient Hindu festival that honors the sun deity, Surya, for its sustenance and blessings. It's celebrated six days after Diwali.

Dress
Dhoti and kurta - Saree

Agriculture workers in Gopalganj, ca. 1915. The traditional dress of Bihari people includes Dhoti and Chapkan (Angarkha) or Kurta (replacing the older chapkan which is a robe fastened on the right or on the left) for men and Saree for women.

Music
Sohar

The traditional music of Bihar is a vibrant reflection of the state's culture and heritage. Sohar: A popular folk song style associated with the birth of a child.

Dance
Kajari

A popular dance in the Bhojpuri-speaking region of Bihar, Kajari is often sung during the rainy season and describes a woman's longing for her lover.

Architecture
Nalanda

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Nalanda University is an archaeological site surrounded by a high wall and large gate.
