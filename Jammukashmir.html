JammuKashmir Culture


Chief Minister-

Governor-Manoj Sinha

Manoj Sinha is an Indian politician serving as the 2nd and the current Lieutenant Governor of Jammu and Kashmir. He served as the Minister of State for Communications and Minister of State for Railways in the Government of India.

Culture and Heritage


Food
Rogan Josh

Rogan Josh is a traditional Kashmiri dish of red meat in a spiced gravy that's slow-cooked to tender perfection. It's a signature dish of Kashmiri cuisine and is often served with steamed rice or naan.

Agriculture
saffron

In Kashmir, saffron is traditionally cultivated in September by planting saffron corms after plough by hand dropping as a mixture of grades under traditional system for longer economic benefits.

Festival
Hemis

Hemis festival is celebrated to commemorate the birthday of Lord Padmasambhava, who is held in high reverence in Tibetan history. Also known as Guru Rinpoche, Lord Padmasambhava was a spiritual leader who threw out demons and evil spirits from Ladakh during the 8th century.

Dress
Pheran

Pheran(Kashmiri pronunciation: [pʰʲaran]) or Phiran is the traditional outfit for both males and females in Kashmir. The pheran consists of two gowns, one over the other.

Music
Sufiana music

Sufiana music, also known as Sufiana Mausiqi, is a classical music form of Kashmir that's a fusion of Persian, Central Asian, and Indian music. It's been performed in the Kashmir valley since the 15th century, when Sufism arrived from Iran.

Dance
Rouf Dance

It is performed by group of women standing face to face to each other but the most notable feature of this dance form is the footwork of the dancers. One of the most known dance form in all the regions of Jammu and Kashmir is 'Rouf.

Architecture
Dogra architecture

Dogra architecture is a style of architecture that was prevalent in Jammu and Kashmir during the rule of the Dogra Hindu kings from 1846 to 1947. It is a unique blend of Hindu, Islamic, and Sikh architectural styles.
