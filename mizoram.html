Culture and Heritage


Food
Koat Pitha

A fried sweet dish made of rice flour and banana, with a soft interior and crispy exterior. It's often served with tea.

Agriculture
Maize

Maize in Mizoram is widely used in various ways likely as a of human food source, livestock feed, and meagre in industrial processing.

Festival
Chapchar Kut

This is the biggest and most important festival in Mizoram. It's celebrated in March after the jhum cultivation is complete, and is marked by singing, dancing, and folk dances.

Dress
Pachhu Puan

Women typically wear a 'Puan' or a wraparound skirt with a matching blouse, while men wear a 'Pachhu' or a long cloth that is draped around the waist and over the shoulder.

Music
Dar hla

These songs are sung for musical instruments, and are named after the instruments they are played on. Dar hla means "song for gong".

Dance
Cheraw

Cheraw is a traditional dance of Mizoram, India, that's also known as the bamboo dance. It's a famous and beautiful dance that's often performed during festive occasions.

Architecture
Zawlbuk

The Zawlbuk was a traditional Mizo bachelors' quarters and social institution that was the largest house in a tribal village.
